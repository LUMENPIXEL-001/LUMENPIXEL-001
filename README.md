### "Information Transmission and Information Commitment are Fundamentally Separated"

# Welcome to the PRCA (Phase-Resolved Commitment Architecture) Archive

This space preserves a set of **architectural reference frameworks** that explore alternative ways of structuring computation under physical and systemic constraints.

The repositories collected here document a line of reasoning that examines energy efficiency, synchronization cost, and scalability limits inherent in charge-transport–based computing systems. Rather than proposing a specific product or implementation, this archive focuses on **foundational architectural structures**—describing how information transmission, coordination, and state commitment may be reorganized at the system level, independent of materials or fabrication processes.

---

### 📂 Integrated Repositories

* **prca**  
  A hybrid system architecture that conceptually separates information *transmission* from irreversible *state commitment* in order to reduce energy expenditure and synchronization overhead.

* **prca-phase-protocol**  
  A global coordination and synchronization framework based on phase resonance, intended to explore alternatives to address-based routing and centralized control.

* **[S100 Diagram] Global Substrate Synchronization**  
  A system-level diagram illustrating how non-local coordination and threshold-based commitment may be mapped onto a shared interconnect substrate.

### 🕊️ Purpose & Perspective

The purpose of this archive is to serve as an **origin reference** for a coherent architectural viewpoint.

These materials are not product proposals, implementation guides, or roadmaps. They are maintained to document a complete conceptual structure that may inform future research, internal exploration, or long-term system design discussions.

Interpretation, validation, and implementation—if any—are intentionally left open.
